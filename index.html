Studies on black holes experienced a breakthrough in 2019, as NASA documented the first image of a black hole. This has been extremely useful considering that our galaxy has its black hole – Sagittarius A. NASA’s twin Voyager probe also discovered the existence of a plasma shield in interstellar space that protects the solar system from cosmic rays (Wall 2019). Astronomers also discovered an intact planet orbiting a dwarf star in late December 2019. It was initially thought the collapse of a star would result in the death of the planet orbiting around it (Wall 2019). However, the discovery showed otherwise. NASA’s Wilkinson Microwave Anisotropy Probe (WMAP) also discovered evidence to support the nearly flat geometry of the universe. This has been useful in resolving the flatness problem associated with the Big Bang theory. The findings of the program were evidence supporting the cosmic inflation theory geometry (Wollack, 2010).

NASA space exploration budget has increased to $81 billion by 2019 and is estimated to continue rising to $93 billion between 2023 and 2027.

A look at the budgetary allocations shows that majority of the budget is spent on science, space operation, and exploration. When it comes to space exploration and operation, orbital infrastructure receives a great amount of funding. This is evident in increased funding in ISS programs and Lunar Orbital Platform-Gateway. Mars exploration is another significant area for NASA – about $1.5 billion is allocated to prepare for launch missions (Boucher 2018). Other deep space exploration activities have received a low budgetary allocation of about $1.4 billion (Boucher 2018).

The expenditure on space exploration has been a subject of debate over the years. Most people argue that the billions spent on space exploration projects could be spent dealing with real-life issues here on earth. However, a fiscal examination of U.S. government expenditure reveals that NASA account for less than one percent of the national budget. For instance, NASA’s $16.143 billion budget in 2007 accounted for 0.58% of the national budget (Brooks 2007). This is a small proportion considering space exploration was allocated about 5% of the national budget in the 1960s. Social programs take more than a trillion of the national budget compared to the meager billions spent on NASA programs.

NASA’s technological contributions have been one of the major justifications for the high budgets. NASA developed the spinoff program to highlight these technologies. GPS single correction software developed by NASA, for instance, has benefitted various industries, including agriculture, telecommunication, and aircraft navigation (NASA 2019). High-pressure tanks that are used by firefighters and in ICU units are also an invention founded on NAS technology (NASA 2019). The aircrafts industry has also benefitted from battery pack technology that was invented as scientists attempted to resolve space flight issues (NASA 2019). Satellite technology, another spinoff of space technology, has benefitted people through its utilization in monitoring the weather and broadcasting television programs.

Existence of Extraterrestrial Life

Despite the advancement in space exploration, one of the issues that remains a hot topic is the existence of extraterrestrial life. Very few people believe in the existence of aliens. Mainstream media may be one of the contributors to this due to the deceptive depiction of extraterrestrial life. Scientists supporting the possibility of extraterrestrial life propose that people should expect the unexpected.

Evidence of the existence of aliens

Sightings of unidentified flying objects (UFOs) are the major evidence of the existence of aliens. In 2017, the New York Times unveiled a Pentagon program that investigated the existence of UFOs (Bartels). Reports of UFO sighting date back to the second millennium BC. There have been reports of fiery discs being observed in the sky. In 2004, a U.S. fighter pilot reported having encountered a UFO that resembled an elongated egg (Picheta 2020). Such sightings are evidence that we are not alone in the universe.
